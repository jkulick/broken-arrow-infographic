Master the Meta: Countering Key Threats

A successful deck isn't just about what you bring; it's about how you neutralize what the enemy brings. Anticipate common threats and build in the tools to dismantle them.

Countering Armored Pushes

Armored spearheads are powerful but vulnerable. Infantry ATGMs excel in urban terrain, while attack helicopters and ATGM jets dominate in the open. Disrupting their recon and support with flanking maneuvers can halt a push before it gains momentum.

Countering Helicopter Rushes

Helicopters are fast and deadly but fragile. Pre-positioned Manpads and SHORAD vehicles create no-fly zones. For reactive threats, Air Superiority Fighters are the ultimate answer, possessing the speed to intercept helicopters anywhere on the map.

Countering Artillery Siege

Artillery is powerful but blind and immobile. The most effective counter is to destroy enemy recon. Failing that, counter-battery fire with your own rocket artillery or a swift strike jet can neutralize the threat directly. Aggressive flanking with light vehicles is also highly effective.

Countering Air Spam

Heavy air power is expensive and has long cooldowns. Layered SAM networks are the primary defense. Proactive deployment of Air Superiority Fighters provides early warning and can effectively dismantle enemy air wings before they can strike.

Define Your Doctrine: Deck Archetypes

Proactive strategies revolve around defined playstyles or "archetypes." Each focuses on a different path to victory, emphasizing certain tactics and unit compositions over others.

This chart compares the core focus of each archetype. Assault decks excel at direct, frontline pressure to control points. Raiding decks use superior maneuverability to strike weak points. Siege decks leverage powerful fire support to grind down defenses from afar. Spearhead decks combine elements to create and exploit a single breakthrough point, while Support decks focus on augmenting allies and disrupting enemy plans with precision tools.

Commander's Insights: Advanced Tactics

Beyond deck composition, effective in-game execution of units and systems can drastically impact the outcome of a match. Master these tactical nuances.

üì°Smart Recon for Strikes

Utilize recon drones (e.g., RQ4) not just for general spotting, but specifically to acquire initial target lock for long-range, high-value assets like cruise missiles or heavy bombers. Even if the recon unit is lost, the initial spot can enable devastating strikes from units kept far from the frontline for safety. This maximizes the value of both your recon and your expensive fire-support.

üîÑFire-and-Move Discipline

For high-end, vulnerable systems like Patriot SAMs, S300, or ATACMS, implement a strict "fire-and-move" protocol. Immediately after launching a missile and neutralizing the threat, **turn off their radar, relocate them, and resupply.** This aggressive repositioning frustrates enemy counter-battery attempts and significantly increases the survivability of your most valuable support assets.

üì¶Strategic Supply Placement

Never cluster supply depots directly adjacent to your high-value units (e.g., ATACMS). If an enemy artillery strike hits a supply depot, the resulting large explosion will likely destroy any nearby expensive systems. Conversely, actively seek out and target enemy supply depots with your own artillery, as their destruction can cripple an opponent's sustained operations.

ü§ùSolo vs. Team Deck Specialization

For solo play, a well-rounded, versatile deck that can adapt to various threats is generally recommended. However, in team play, consider specializing your deck. One player might focus on defensive assets (TOWs, Stingers, SAMs), while another builds an offensive deck (infantry, fast vehicles, recon). This allows for greater synergy and efficiency in coordinated team strategies.

‚úàÔ∏èAir Superiority is Key

When relying on air assets, especially bombers or strike jets, ensure your deck includes sufficient Air Superiority Fighters (ASFs). If the enemy gains air superiority, your high-value offensive air units can be denied from entering the battlefield or quickly destroyed. ASFs are crucial for protecting your investment and ensuring your air strategy can be executed effectively.

‚ö°SEAD for Air Dominance

SEAD (Suppression of Enemy Air Defense) jets are vital for dismantling enemy radar-guided anti-air systems. These jets carry anti-radiation missiles designed to outrange and neutralize enemy SAMs, creating safe corridors for your strike jets and bombers to operate without being immediately shot down. Prioritize SEAD to establish and maintain air dominance.
