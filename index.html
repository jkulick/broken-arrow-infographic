Know Your Arsenal: The Six Unit Tabs

Your 10,000-point budget is distributed across six distinct unit categories, or 'Tabs'. Each tab serves a unique purpose on the battlefield. A balanced deck understands the role and value of each.

üî≠Recon

The eyes of your army. Provides essential vision to enable long-range fire support and prevent ambushes. This tab is the exclusive home of all drones. Key units like Diversanty (with Stealth 3 Informers) or the elite Spetsnaz GRU exemplify effective scouting.

üèÉInfantry

Versatile ground-pounders. Excellent for holding positions, close-quarters combat, and specialized anti-tank (ATGM) or anti-air (MANPADS) roles. Units like AGS-17 excel against exposed infantry, while PTO Metis and PTRK Kornet provide potent AT options.

üõ°Ô∏èVehicles

The backbone of direct fire. This tab contains your main battle tanks, ATGM carriers, and engineering vehicles designed for frontline assault. Wheeled options like BTR-82 and K16-Bumerang offer speed and versatility, with some capable of APS.

üéØSupport

The key to sustainability and denial. Home to air defense (SAMs, SHORAD), artillery, and critical logistics vehicles that rearm and repair your forces. Grad and Smerch provide heavy artillery, while Osa and S-300 are crucial AA elements. Logistics like Ural 4320/5323 are vital.

üöÅHelicopters

Masters of rapid response. Helicopters ignore terrain to provide swift transport, devastating fire support, and quick reaction force capabilities. Gunpods are now effective, allowing for anti-heli or anti-infantry roles. Transport helis are key for rapid supply/troop deployment.

‚úàÔ∏èAir

Apex predators of the battlefield. Fixed-wing aircraft deliver unparalleled speed and firepower, from air superiority fighters to ground-pounding strike jets. SEAD is almost always a must-have for any deck to clear enemy AA. The Su-25T offers versatile options.

Master the Meta: Countering Key Threats

A successful deck isn't just about what you bring; it's about how you neutralize what the enemy brings. Anticipate common threats and build in the tools to dismantle them.

Countering Armored Pushes

Armored spearheads are powerful but vulnerable. Infantry ATGMs excel in urban terrain, while attack helicopters and ATGM jets dominate in the open. Disrupting their recon and support with flanking maneuvers can halt a push before it gains momentum.

Countering Helicopter Rushes

Helicopters are fast and deadly but fragile. Pre-positioned Manpads and SHORAD vehicles create no-fly zones. For reactive threats, Air Superiority Fighters are the ultimate answer, possessing the speed to intercept helicopters anywhere on the map.

Countering Artillery Siege

Artillery is powerful but blind and immobile. The most effective counter is to destroy enemy recon. Failing that, counter-battery fire with your own rocket artillery or a swift strike jet can neutralize the threat directly. Aggressive flanking with light vehicles is also highly effective. Avoid firing MLRS inside forests or immediately in front of very tall buildings to prevent self-damage.

Countering Air Spam

Heavy air power is expensive and has long cooldowns. Layered SAM networks are the primary defense. Proactive deployment of Air Superiority Fighters provides early warning and can effectively dismantle enemy air wings before they can strike.

Define Your Doctrine: Deck Archetypes

Proactive strategies revolve around defined playstyles or "archetypes." Each focuses on a different path to victory, emphasizing certain tactics and unit compositions over others.

This chart compares the core focus of each archetype. Assault decks excel at direct, frontline pressure to control points. Raiding decks use superior maneuverability to strike weak points. Siege decks leverage powerful fire support to grind down defenses from afar. Spearhead decks combine elements to create and exploit a single breakthrough point, while Support decks focus on augmenting allies and disrupting enemy plans with precision tools.

Tactical Insights: In-Game Execution

Effective in-game execution of units and systems can drastically impact the outcome of a match. Master these tactical nuances.

üéÆMicro-Management: Precision & Movement

Don't Clump Units: Avoid simply attack-moving large groups. Zoom in and micro-manage individual units or utilize command groups for precise control and better unit preservation.
Fast Move vs. Regular Move: Right-clicking twice initiates a "fast move," which prioritizes roads but can make units more vulnerable. A single right-click will move units in a straight line, regardless of terrain, which can be safer for avoiding enemy line of sight or ambushes.

üëÅÔ∏èLine of Sight (LOS) & Vision Control

Scout Aggressively: Use fast, cheap units (like IFVs or jeeps) to scout ahead and establish LOS on enemy positions. Early vision is crucial for enabling your long-range assets.
Utilize Terrain and Cover: Trees, buildings, and other terrain features provide cover and can significantly improve your LOS advantage while simultaneously denying the enemy vision. Mastering map terrain is key to effective vision control.

üî≠Optics & Stealth Mechanics

Optics: Determines how far a unit can see. Standard optics for most units is 1200m.
Stealth: The opposite of optics, hindering enemy vision.
Detection Distance: Optics / Stealth = distance seen. E.g., a 1200 Optics unit sees a Stealth 3 Recon at 400m (1200/3).
Terrain Impact: Trees and buildings multiply a unit's Stealth value by 2, significantly reducing detection range. They also reduce incoming damage by 2x.
"Flash Effect": A unit's Stealth value is temporarily lowered when it fires. Repeated firing stacks this "flash effect," making the unit easier to spot. This effect fades if the unit ceases fire.

üõ°Ô∏èArmor vs. Penetration

Kinetic Damage:
- If Penetration (Pen) equals Armor, 100% damage is dealt (e.g., Pen 500 vs. Armor 500 = 80 dmg).
- If Pen is half of Armor, only 50% damage is dealt (e.g., Pen 250 vs. Armor 500 = 40 dmg).
Explosive/HE/HEAT Damage: Always deals some damage in a bell-curve distribution. At less than half/equal/twice the target's armor value relative to penetration, damage is approximately 20%/50%/80% respectively.
Top Armor Hits: Some weapons hit "Top" armor, which is a different, often more deadly, mechanic as top armor is typically thinner.
Active Protection Systems (APS): APS has a limited number of "charges" and can only trigger once every 5 seconds. This cooldown is crucial to exploit.

üí®Smoke Usage: Offense & Defense

Line of Sight (LoS) Break: Smoke immediately breaks LoS.
Deflect Projectiles: Pop smoke to deflect incoming projectiles, especially when an enemy is about to fire a high-impact shot.
Retreat Safely: For tanks, reverse-move them out of smoke to maintain their frontal armor facing while retreating, allowing them to fire on pursuing enemies.
Sources: Some infantry and vehicles can deploy smoke (uses must be resupplied). Artillery can also lay down smoke in three different patterns.
Tactical Use: Use smoke to cover advances, retreats, or to temporarily blind enemy positions, especially those with powerful long-range fire. Be aware that firing within smoke can reveal your position due to the "flash effect."

üèÉ‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èSprint (Movement Modifier)

Effect: Sprint temporarily increases infantry speed, negating "Crouching" (Yellow) and "Prone" (Red) modifiers. Can be used offensively to charge or defensively to break suppression and get to cover.
Limitations: Units cannot fire any weapon while Sprinting. Sprint is disabled when units are inside buildings. Sprint has a cooldown period after use.
Building Destruction: If a building containing enemy infantry is destroyed, the game performs a roll to determine if the units inside are killed.

üî•Fire Control: Hold Fire (H) vs. Attack Move (Q)

Hold Fire (H): A unit will only fire if it can see an enemy AND that enemy is about to fire at them. This is useful for maintaining stealth for recon units or preserving ammunition.
Attack Move (Q): A unit will stop and aim as soon as it spots an enemy, provided it can penetrate or damage that enemy. This is for aggressive engagement and general forward movement with auto-targeting.

üí•Overkill Mechanic (Right Click)

Purpose: Right-clicking on an enemy unit initiates an "Overkill" command. Your unit will attempt to expend all its munitions to destroy the target.
Against APS/AA: This is highly effective against vehicles with Active Protection Systems (APS) or anti-air (AA) systems that have cooldowns (e.g., 5 seconds for APS). By "overkilling," you can land multiple hits in rapid succession during the APS cooldown window, ensuring destruction. This applies to SEAD engagements as well.

üì¶Supply Lines: The Logistics Backbone

Tiered Supply Vehicles: Deploy a large supply vehicle (e.g., 10,000-20,000 supply capacity like Ural 4320/5323) in a safe rear area as your main hub. Use smaller vehicles (e.g., 2,000-4,000 supply capacity) to ferry supplies from this main hub to your front lines and artillery positions.
Avoid Clumping: Never place artillery or anti-air units directly near your main supply hub. Supply dumps are extremely volatile and explode if hit, potentially destroying nearby valuable units.

üö∂‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏èInfantry Tactics: Ground Force Proficiency

Situational Use: Infantry should be used situationally. Deploy them in large numbers primarily after other elements of your force (recon, initial fire support) are established to secure ground or clear buildings.
Guerrilla Tactics: Utilize infantry to probe enemy lines, engage briefly, then retreat and regroup. This can bait enemy units into unfavorable positions or reveal their deployment, allowing you to punish overextended opponents.
Efficient Deployment: Always utilize transports (trucks, helicopters, or planes) to deploy infantry quickly and efficiently across the map. Infantry on foot are extremely slow and vulnerable.

General Strategic Pointers

Beyond specific tactics, these overarching principles will guide your strategic decision-making throughout the game.

üß†Know Your Units & The Meta

Deeply understand the strengths, weaknesses, and optimal roles of every unit in your deck, as well as common enemy units. Keep abreast of the current game meta to anticipate prevalent strategies and counter them effectively.

üó∫Ô∏èAdapt to the Map

Broken Arrow maps are based on real-world locations, each offering unique terrain features (urban, forested, open, chokepoints). Adapt your deployment, movement, and engagements to leverage terrain advantages and exploit enemy vulnerabilities based on the map's layout.

ü§ùCoordinate with Your Team

In multiplayer, effective communication is paramount. Coordinate with teammates to cover different sectors, share vision, combine fire, and execute synchronized pushes or defenses. Utilize in-game markers for quick communication.

‚è≥Patience & Reinforcement Flow

Reinforcements take time to arrive on the battlefield. Plan your attacks and defenses accordingly, allowing sufficient time for units to deploy and move into position. Avoid overextending without knowing when your next wave of support will arrive.

Key Bindings & Shortcuts

Mastering these keyboard shortcuts will significantly improve your efficiency and reaction time on the battlefield, enabling quicker commands and better control of your units.

Movement & Engagement

  • Right-Click (Single): Regular Move (straight line, respects terrain)
  • Right-Click (Double): Fast Move (prioritizes roads, faster but more vulnerable)
  • Q: Attack Move (unit moves, stops to engage spotted enemies)
  • H: Hold Fire (unit only fires if attacked)
  • Right-Click (on enemy): Overkill (unit expends all munitions to destroy target)
  • X: Sprint (infantry speed boost)
  • B: Back to Base (refund units)

  • F2: Attack Marker

  • (No specific key): Radar Activation (via action panel)
  • Left-Click (Decoy button): Activate Decoy

  • Ctrl+Click (Supply card): Buy 4000 Kg Supplies

Battlefield Topography: Leveraging Map Types

Different maps in Broken Arrow present unique strategic challenges and opportunities. Understanding the terrain and its impact on unit effectiveness is crucial for victory.

For detailed information and visuals of all available maps, visit the Broken Arrow Map Armory.

üèôÔ∏èUrban & Forested Terrain

Stealth & Damage Reduction: Buildings and dense forests provide significant cover, multiplying unit stealth by 2x and reducing incoming damage by 2x. This makes infantry and high-stealth recon extremely powerful in these environments.
Close-Quarters Combat: Favors infantry with CQC weapons (e.g., Shotguns, Flamethrowers, heavy RPGs) and light vehicles with autocannons. Tanks struggle with restricted line of sight and vulnerability to close-range AT from multiple angles.

üèûÔ∏èOpen & Mixed Terrain

Armor & Long Range: Open maps favor armored vehicles, long-range ATGMs, and air assets, which can fully exploit their range and mobility. Recon is vital for vision control in wide-open spaces.
Artillery Effectiveness: Artillery is particularly effective on open maps where units are more exposed and easier to pinpoint, especially with good recon support.
Maneuver Warfare: Allows for large-scale flanking maneuvers and rapid advances, making wheeled vehicles and helicopters highly effective for exploitation.

üåâChokepoints & Bridges

Defense Advantage: Maps with prominent chokepoints (like bridges or narrow valleys) offer strong defensive opportunities. Units with high frontal armor, explosive damage, or area-denial capabilities (e.g., heavy tanks, mortars, heavy machine guns) excel here.
Vulnerability: While defensible, chokepoints are vulnerable to heavy artillery barrages or precision air strikes that can clear defenders and deny passage. Smoke can be critical for forcing an advance or retreat through these areas.

üö©Control Point Significance

Victory Condition: Control points are the primary objective. Their placement on the map often dictates the flow of engagement.
Strategic Value: Consider if control points are in urban areas (favoring infantry), open fields (favoring tanks/long-range AT), or chokepoints (requiring strong defensive setups). Your deck should be tailored to efficiently capture and defend the types of points presented on a given map.
